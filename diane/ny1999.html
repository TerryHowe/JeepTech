Happy New Year!
From Terry and Diane Howe

As we approach the new millennium at warp speed (it seems like I just sat down to write last year's Christmas letter), I can't think of anything better than to think of all the loved ones in our lives and wish you all a Happy New Year!

Speaking of warp speed, you're not speaking of Terry's 1945 CJ2A which he finished putting together earlier this year and has been 4-wheeling ever since.  Nor are you speaking of Diane's 1959 Studebaker that she got in September.  No matter, speed isn't the only thing.    Diane plans to cut her teeth on the simple machinery of the Studebaker and Terry, well you know, Terry is a total jeep-aholic.  In any event, Diane and Terry will have vehicles that are drivable on New Years Day.

Speaking of which Terry and Diane are taking this Y2K bug very seriously (don't believe it for a minute).  Everyone is invited to their New Year's Eve bash this year and plenty of candles and flashlights are on hand.  Diane has been stockpiling champagne ever since her vacation to France earlier this year.  Yes, very serious indeed.

Terry and Diane both left MCI in April.  Terry is in a little company that does voice over internet software switches and Diane is at a company called Concert.  Concert just became legally cleared as a joint global venture between British Telecom and AT&T, so it is like she is "almost" working for AT&T again.  It also serves her yen for traveling since some travel (to England and Virginia ) is involved.  Terry's appetite for being a hard core software developer is being sated at Vsys.

Terry achieved two badges of honor for any serious jeeper this year.  One is wheeling the Rubicon in California in the summer, the other is rolling on the side his CJ2A on an extreme trail here in Colorado (no injuries, except pride).  He is busy participating in two 4-wheeling clubs and helping start a new online magazine venture at www.jeepwire.com.   Diane is serving the local Society of Women Engineers section as president this year and also organizing a project to develop an engineering web site made possible by a grant from Exxon Education Foundation.

Together, Terry and Diane play coed soccer together and just love playing together in Colorado.  Come out and join us anytime!
