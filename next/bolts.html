Using the proper strength fastener for the job can be a life saver. During my restoration work of the Enola Gay, I had to do a lot of research on fasteners. Fortunately, selection of fasteners for the four wheeler is not quite as crucial as it is in aviation industry.

The following chart indicates that a grade 5 or 8 should be fine for tow hooks since two of them are used together. These strength ratings in the chart are minimum reqirements with a safety factor.

These are other specialized aircraft grades that go up past 220,000psi, but they are too expensive for practical automotive use:

  • Supertanium,160,000psi, 8 points on head, quenched/tempered special alloy steel.
  • A354BD, A490, 150,000psi, no markings, carbon quenched/tempered alloy steel.
  • NAS144, (Military and Aircraft std)-160,000psi
  • Aircraft Assigned Steel, no number, 220,000psi, high carbon alloy
and the list goes on.....

Grade 8's should be plenty strong enough for any automotive use. You are more likely to rip the fastener from the frame than have a grade 8 snap on you. Remember though, this is only true for new bolts, torqued to the correct specification.

For 1/2" bolt:
Grade 5's 78 ft/lbs, no lubrication.
Grade 8's 119 ft/lbs, no lubrication

If there is grease/oil on the fastener, decrease the torque 30% for low lubricity and 50% for high lubricity.
